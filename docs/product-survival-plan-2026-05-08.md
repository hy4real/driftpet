# driftpet Survival Plan

## Premise

driftpet does not get to survive just because the concept is elegant.

It only survives if one narrow loop becomes strong enough that the user reaches for it naturally.

Based on the current codebase and product shape, that loop should be:

> continuity: driftpet becomes the best place to resume a broken thread.

Not reminder.
Not ambient companionship alone.
Not generic summarization.

## The Rule

For the next phase, every feature must answer one question:

> Does this make driftpet materially better at restoring a user's interrupted thread?

If not, it is secondary at best and distracting at worst.

## Keep

These pieces already support the continuity bet and should remain in the core:

### 1. Chaos-reset workbench

Why keep it:

- it is the cleanest current capture surface,
- it tolerates messy thought,
- it already points toward thread re-entry instead of generic chat.

What it means:

- this stays central,
- it should become the primary input story,
- it should be framed as "save the line before it disappears."

### 2. Compact next-move card

Why keep it:

- the product still needs compression,
- a single next move is more useful than a broad reflection,
- it keeps the pet actionable.

What it means:

- cards should become more specific, not more elaborate,
- the card should favor re-entry over explanation.

### 3. Restrained related recall

Why keep it:

- continuity needs memory,
- but weak memory is worse than no memory,
- the current direction already prefers empty recall over noisy recall.

What it means:

- maintain strict filtering,
- prefer one strong thread anchor over multiple weak associations.

### 4. Lightweight pet presence

Why keep it:

- the pet metaphor still matters,
- presence helps thread continuity feel externalized,
- but presence must support utility, not replace it.

What it means:

- keep stance, calm motion, and remembered-thread hints,
- stop treating presentation as the main unlock.

## Cut Or De-Emphasize

These are not necessarily bad. They are just not where the product earns survival.

### 1. Telegram as the hero story

Why de-emphasize it:

- it is an ingestion path, not the product thesis,
- forwarding something from your phone is useful, but not what makes the pet indispensable.

Decision:

- keep it as an optional lane,
- stop treating it as the main reason the product exists.

### 2. Status / health surfaces as foreground value

Why de-emphasize them:

- they help development and trust,
- they do not justify the app.

Decision:

- keep them operational,
- do not spend product energy making them feel like core value.

### 3. More cosmetic aliveness

Why cut it from priority:

- the app already has copy, mood, glance, breathing, and remembered-thread presence,
- none of those fix the weak utility loop by themselves.

Decision:

- no more "alive" polish unless it directly improves thread restoration.

### 4. Broad URL / capture expansion

Why de-emphasize it:

- more capture surfaces do not help if re-entry is still weak,
- breadth can hide the fact that the core loop is still optional.

Decision:

- only expand capture when it clearly improves timing or reduces friction for continuity.

## Prove

The next phase must prove continuity with concrete behavior, not positioning language.

### What must become true

When the user returns after interruption, driftpet should be the fastest trustworthy way to answer:

- what was I actually doing,
- where did the thread break,
- what should I do next,
- what can I safely ignore for now.

### What the product needs

Not more inputs.
Not more summaries.

It needs a stronger resume lane.

That likely means:

- a clearly visible "last real thread" slot,
- a cleaner handoff between chaos dump and resumable thread,
- better storage of the exact next step and set-aside context,
- less emphasis on the full card archive and more emphasis on the one thread that matters now.

## Working Standard

If a user can resume from their own scattered notes just as easily as from driftpet, driftpet loses.

If a user can recover with ten seconds of internal self-talk just as easily as with driftpet, driftpet loses.

If driftpet becomes the most reliable external place to pick a broken thread back up, it wins the right to keep existing.

## Suggested Phase Order

### Phase 1 — Commit to continuity

- treat continuity as the primary product claim,
- stop broadening the product story.

### Phase 2 — Simplify the surface around resume

- make the current thread more prominent than the archive,
- reduce distractions around non-core pet behaviors,
- bias the UI toward "resume this line."

### Phase 3 — Validate with real interrupted sessions

- use real work sessions,
- check whether the user actually re-enters through driftpet,
- reject polite-but-non-habitual usage as failure.

## Kill Condition

If driftpet still feels optional after a focused continuity pass, the honest move is not more polish.

The honest move is to either:

- pivot the product around a different core claim,
- or stop pretending the current shape is strong enough.
